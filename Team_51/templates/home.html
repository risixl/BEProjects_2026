Understanding Brain Tumors

Brain tumors are abnormal growths of cells in the brain that can be either benign (non-cancerous) or malignant (cancerous). Early detection is crucial for effective treatment and better patient outcomes.

Glioma

The most common type of brain tumor, arising from glial cells that support nerve cells.

Meningioma

Usually benign tumors that develop from the meninges, the protective membranes around the brain.

Pituitary

Tumors that develop in the pituitary gland, often affecting hormone production.

No Tumor

Normal brain tissue with no abnormal growths detected in the MRI scan.
