About Monkeypox

Monkeypox is a viral zoonotic disease with symptoms similar to smallpox, though clinically less severe. It's caused by the monkeypox virus and can spread from animals to humans as well as between people.

Our detection system uses advanced machine learning algorithms to analyze symptoms and skin lesion images to identify potential monkeypox cases, helping with early diagnosis and treatment.

Early detection is crucial for managing outbreaks and preventing further transmission of the virus.

Common Symptoms

Fever

One of the first symptoms, often accompanied by headache, muscle aches, and exhaustion.

Headache

Severe headaches are common in the initial stage of infection.

Swollen Lymph Nodes

This symptom distinguishes monkeypox from other similar diseases like smallpox.

Skin Rash

Rash typically appears 1-3 days after fever, often beginning on the face then spreading.

Prevention Guidelines

Hand Hygiene

Wash hands regularly with soap and water or use alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

Avoid Contact

Avoid close contact with people who have symptoms or confirmed monkeypox.

Vaccination

Consider vaccination if you're at high risk of exposure to monkeypox.

Protective Gear

Use appropriate personal protective equipment when caring for infected individuals.
